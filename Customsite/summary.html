    • A Summary of the History of The Everglades.

    The everglades strech almost 11,000 miles from Kissimee, to Okeechobee, and Biscayne Bay. They provide water to almost 7 million floridans and is one of the largest biodiverse ecosystems in the state. They were declared a national park in 1947 by President Harry Truman to protect the natural landscape and the prevent degradation of the ecosystem. Today, the Everglades are threatened by pollution, human expansion, invasive species, and erosion. Many organizations have been created to help protect them, but Florida legislators are not focusing on protecting them.
